# EU Digital Product Passport - Regulatory Context

This document provides context about the EU Digital Product Passport (DPP) and how this Pimcore implementation aligns with regulatory requirements.

## Overview

The **Digital Product Passport** is a key component of the EU's **Ecopolicy Sustainability Requirements (ESPR)** framework, designed to provide consumers and supply chain actors with transparent, verified information about a product's environmental and social characteristics.

---

## Key EU Regulations

### 1. ESPR - Ecopolicy Sustainability Requirements
**Regulation:** (EU) 2024/XX (Emerging/Proposed)

**Objectives:**
- Increase transparency of product environmental footprint
- Combat greenwashing
- Enable circular economy transition
- Empower consumers with sustainability information

**Key Components:**
- Mandatory energy/environmental labeling
- Digital product passport requirements
- Durability & repairability standards
- Restrictions on destruction of unsold goods

---

### 2. Ecopolicy Directive
**Regulation:** (EU) 2024/XX (Proposed)

**Applies to:**
- Durable products
- Textiles and fashion
- Electronics
- Furniture
- Lighting devices

**Requirements:**
- 5-10 year durability guarantees
- Availability of spare parts
- Repair information accessibility
- Environmental footprint declarations

---

### 3. Textiles Strategy & Digital Identity
**EU Reference:** European Commission Circular Economy Action Plan

**Textile-Specific Requirements:**
- Material composition transparency
- Chemical usage disclosure
- Supply chain traceability
- Carbon & water footprint calculations
- End-of-life recyclability information

## DPP Requirements for Fashion/Textiles

### Mandatory Information (Required by 2027-2028)

#### 1. **Product Identification**
- Product name
- SKU / Batch number
- Product category
- Manufacturer identification
- Model variant details

#### 2. **Material Composition**
- List of all materials (ISO 1043 naming)
- Percentage composition
- Origin country of each material
- Certifications per material (GOTS, OEKO-TEX, etc.)

#### 3. **Environmental Footprint**
- **Carbon footprint** (kg CO2 equivalent)
  - Scope: Production, transportation, packaging
  - Methodology: ISO 14040/44
  
- **Water consumption** (liters)
  - Agriculture/cultivation
  - Processing and dyeing
  
- **Chemical usage**
  - List of all dyes used
  - Chemical treatments
  - Hazardous substance disclosures (SVHC)

#### 4. **Durability & Repairability**
- Estimated lifespan (months/years)
- Durability test results
- Repairability score (0-10)
- Availability of spare parts
- Link to repair/care instructions

#### 5. **End-of-Life & Recyclability**
- Recyclability score (0-100)
- Recycling instructions
- Take-back/collection programs
- Material recovery pathways

#### 6. **Compliance & Certifications**
- List of certifications (GOTS, OEKO-TEX, Fair Trade, etc.)
- Standards compliance (EU directives, ISO standards)
- Chemical compliance (REACH, CLP, RoHS)
- Labor standards (SA8000, Fair Trade)

#### 7. **Supply Chain Transparency**
- Origin of raw materials
- Production locations (country level minimum)
- Key processing steps and locations
- Certifications per supply chain step
- Labor condition information

### Recommended Information

- **Biodiversity impact** - Water stress, pesticide use impacts
- **Social metrics** - Fair wages, working conditions
- **Longevity data** - Detailed wear resistance tests
- **Alternative materials** - Available eco-friendly options
- **Packaging information** - Material and recyclability
- **Instructions for use** - Care, washing, maintenance

### Optional Information

- **Detailed LCA reports** - Full lifecycle assessment
- **Third-party audits** - Supply chain verification
- **Blockchain/QR data** - Traceability links
- **Video content** - Care and repair tutorials
- **Social impact metrics** - Community programs

## Mandatory Standards & Methodologies

### Environmental Footprint Calculation
- **ISO 14040/44** - Life Cycle Assessment (LCA)
- **ISO 14062** - Environmental management in product design
- **Product Environmental Footprint (PEF)** - EU methodology

### Water Impact Assessment
- **Water Footprint Network** - Methodology
- ISO 14046 - Water footprint assessment

### Carbon Footprint
- **ISO 14064** - Greenhouse gas quantification
- **GHG Protocol** - Corporate standard
- **Scope 1, 2, 3** - Emission categorization

### Textile Testing Standards
- **ISO 3048** - Textile fiber classification
- **ISO 105** - Color fastness testing
- **ISO 20344** - Footwear performance testing

## Certifications Aligned with EU Regulations

### Organic & Sustainable Materials
- **GOTS** (Global Organic Textile Standard)
  - Most rigorous organic cotton standard
  - Covers production, processing, labeling
  
- **OEKO-TEX Standard 100**
  - Chemical safety in textiles
  - Mandatory for ESPR compliance
  
- **Global Recycled Standard (GRS)**
  - Verifies recycled content percentage
  - Required for recycled material claims

### Environmental Management
- **ISO 14001** - Environmental management systems
- **EMAS** - EU Eco-Management and Audit Scheme

### Social Responsibility
- **SA8000** - Social accountability
- **Fair Trade Certified** - Labor & trade practices
- **B Corp Certified** - Comprehensive sustainability

### Chemical Compliance
- **ZDHC Level 4** - Zero Discharge of Hazardous Chemicals
- **REACH Compliant** - EU chemical regulation
- **Bluesign®** - Textile production chemicals

## Non-Compliance Penalties

### For Manufacturers/Importers
- **Warning letters** - Initial non-compliance
- **Fines:** Up to €10,000-50,000 per violation
- **Product seizure** - Removal from market
- **Repeat violations:** Up to €100,000+ fines
- **Legal proceedings** - Court action possible

### For Distributors
- **Liability** - If false DPP data provided
- **Fine sharing** - Proportional responsibility
- **Market removal** - De-listing of products

### Enforcement
- **National authorities** - Enforcement agencies per EU member
- **Consumer protection boards** - Verify claims
- **Market surveillance** - Regular audits
- **Whistleblower protections** - Report violations

## Implementation Best Practices

### For Manufacturers
1. **Collect data** from suppliers
2. **Validate accuracy** against standards
3. **Keep records** for 5+ years
4. **Update annually** or when product changes
5. **Test compliance** against JSON Schema
6. **Monitor regulations** for updates

### For Platform Operators
1. **Implement validation** - Schema compliance
2. **Secure data** - Encryption & authentication
3. **Audit logs** - Track all changes
4. **Performance** - Handle large-scale queries
5. **Scalability** - Multi-tenant architecture
6. **API versioning** - Support older formats

### Data Quality Checks
```
✓ All required fields present
✓ Material percentages sum to 100%
✓ Environmental metrics realistic
✓ Certifications valid & current
✓ Country codes ISO-3166 compliant
✓ Dates in ISO 8601 format
✓ URLs accessible & valid
```

## FAQ

**Q: Is DPP mandatory now?**
A: Not yet for textiles. Phase 1 (electronics) begins 2026. Phase 2 (textiles) 2027-2028.

**Q: What if we don't comply?**
A: Products cannot be legally sold in EU. Fines up to €50,000+ per violation.

**Q: Can we estimate environmental metrics?**
A: Only during transition period. Final implementation requires verified data.

**Q: Do we need to disclose suppliers?**
A: Supply chain step transparency yes, but specific supplier names optional (for SME protection).

**Q: How often must DPP be updated?**
A: Annually minimum, or immediately if product/compliance status changes.

**Q: Can we use data from other DPP systems?**
A: Yes, if data is current and verifiable. Document the source.
